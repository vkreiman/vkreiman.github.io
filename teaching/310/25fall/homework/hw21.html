Homework 21: Covariance

  1. Section 4.6, #5, 11.
  2. Suppose that \(X\) and \(Y\) have joint pdf $$ f_{X,Y}(x,y)= \begin{cases} \frac{4}{5}(xy+1), & 0\lt x\lt 1, 0\lt y\lt 1\\ 0, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$ Find Cov\((X,Y)\), the covariance of \(X\) and \(Y\).
  3. Let \(X\) be uniformly distributed on \([-1,1]\), and let \(Y=X^2\).
    1. Show that Cov\((X,Y)=0\).
    2. Prove that \(X\) and \(Y)\ are not independent.
    Note that we've shown in class that the following holds: if two random variables are independent, then their covariance is 0. This problem illustrates that the converse of this statement is false.
  4. Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be independent, each with mean 2 and variance 1. Let \(U=3X+2Y\), \(V=2X-3Y\). Find Var \(U\) and Cov\((U,V)\).
